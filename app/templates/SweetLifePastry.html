Sweet Life Pastry

Family owned Mexican Bakery.

What to get: Tres Leches Strawberry Vanilla Cake.

Restaurant Link: Sweet Life Pastry Website

Menu Link: Sweet Life Pastry Menu

More details: Our favorite part about Sweet Life Pastry is the family-owned atmosphere. There's multiple generations of family members working the kitchen, tables, and register here, and that makes it a really fun place to be. We got the tres leches cake, because we wanted to get something relatively Mexican, since they specialize in Mexican baked goods at Sweet Life Pastry. This cake was really, really delicious! Somehow they'd soaked the cake layers in sweet milks, but the cake didn't lose it's body or texture. The cake was still light and fluffy, yet it was also moist and rich with the milk. It was vanilla cake with strawberry frosting, and the frosting had realy strawberries mixed into it. This helped give the relatively rich cake a lighter, fresher taste. We love frosting, and would be happy with a big tub of frosting, but this cake had what felt like a perfect, moderate amount of frosting: enough to make it delicious, but not so much as to overpower the cake. We had hoped to get the churros that we saw recommended on Yelp, but they were all out. We got here around 2pm on a Sunday, and it seemed like much of their dessert foods had been depleted from a busy morning of selling. We recommend trying to get here earlier in the day if you want to see a full selection of their best foods, but it'll be delicious here whenever you arrive.
